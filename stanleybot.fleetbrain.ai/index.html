Transparency

What goes wrong.
And what we do about it.

Nobody else puts their mistakes on their marketing site. We do, because the corrections are the product.

Stan recommended a vendor before checking reviews. A real person got nervous. The operator caught it and reversed the sequence permanently. Stan checks reputation first now — every time, for every client.

Stan used presentation formatting — headers, pitch-deck energy — when a real person asked a real question. The operator killed the sales tone. Stan talks like a person now.

Stan presented a vacation comparison from memory instead of doing live research. The numbers were estimates, not facts. The operator called it out. Stan now distinguishes between live research and general knowledge.

Stan found a $63,670 gap in a partnership's finances but mislabeled the waterfall — making profitable scenarios look like losses. The operator caught it. Stan rebuilt the analysis correctly at the same URL.

Every mistake makes the system better. A system that doesn't fail doesn't learn. A system that fails without someone catching it doesn't improve. The human operator is the difference.
